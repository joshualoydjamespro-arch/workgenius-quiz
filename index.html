THE SIX TYPES OF WORKING GENIUS

🌀
Wonder

The genius of curiosity and possibility. People with Wonder naturally ask “What if?” and spot gaps, opportunities, and unmet potential that others overlook. They live in the questions and the big picture, identifying that something could be better before anyone else has named it. This is the upstream spark of vision and strategic thinking.

💡
Invention

The genius of creation from scratch. People with Invention thrive on blank pages and the challenge of generating new ideas, solutions, products, or approaches that didn’t exist before. They enjoy ambiguity and the process of turning possibilities into something tangible. This is the creative engine that turns vision into reality.

🧠
Discernment

The genius of wise judgment and intuition. People with Discernment have a natural ability to evaluate ideas, plans, and situations and quickly sense what will work, what won’t, and why. They synthesize patterns and provide trustworthy guidance that helps teams avoid wasted effort on the wrong paths.

📣
Galvanizing

The genius of inspiration and momentum. People with Galvanizing naturally rally others around a vision or goal, creating buy-in, energy, and collective movement. They turn individual agreement into team action and help good ideas gain traction through clear communication and enthusiasm.

🤝
Enablement

The genius of support and practical help. People with Enablement anticipate what others need to succeed and provide it — removing obstacles, offering resources, encouragement, and behind-the-scenes support. They are often the glue that holds teams and projects together, making everyone else more effective.

🏁
Tenacity

The genius of persistence and completion. People with Tenacity have the grit to push through obstacles, grind through details, and drive things to the finish line. They ensure that good ideas actually become completed realities, even when the work is unglamorous or difficult.

Why Knowing Your Profile Changes Everything

Everyone has all six types to some degree, but you have two Geniuses where you thrive with natural energy and joy, two Competencies you can do well but that cost you energy, and two Frustrations that drain you significantly.

When you spend most of your time in your Geniuses, work feels fulfilling and sustainable. When you’re stuck in your Frustrations, burnout and frustration rise — even if you’re “good” at the task. Great teams have all six represented. Knowing your profile (and others’) lets you play to strengths, delegate wisely, and communicate needs clearly.

The Six Types of Working Genius

Created by Patrick Lencioni and The Table Group, this model identifies six distinct types of “genius” that every successful project needs. No one person has all six as geniuses. You have two where you thrive with energy (Geniuses), two you can do but that cost energy (Competencies), and two that drain you (Frustrations).

WON Wonder — Curiosity, asking why, seeing possibilities and gaps.
INV Invention — Creating new ideas, solutions, and innovations from scratch.
DIS Discernment — Intuition, judgment, sensing what will work.
GAL Galvanizing — Rallying and inspiring others to action.
ENA Enablement — Supporting others and removing obstacles so they succeed.
TEN Tenacity — Pushing through, finishing, and driving to completion.

The goal: Spend more time in your Geniuses, protect yourself from Frustrations, and build teams where all six types are present and valued.
